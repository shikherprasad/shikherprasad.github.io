Continuing from the last post, where we discussed how whatsApp achieves end-to-end encryption, we will discuss how WhatsApp  achieves end-to-end encryption in media and other attachments transmission.

The sender generates an ephemeral 32-byte AES256 key, and an ephemeral 32-byte HMAC-SHA256 key. It uses the AES-256 key to encrypt the message and uses the SHA-256 key to append the MAC* of the ciphertext.
*MAC (message authentication code) is a short piece of information used to authenticate a message, i.e.,  to confirm that the message came from the stated sender (its authenticity) and has not been changed in transit.
Then the sender uploads the encrypted attachment to a blob (Binary Large OBject) store and transmits the encrypted message to the recipient that contains the encryption keys and a pointer to the blob in the blob store.

The recipient retrieves the encrypted blob from the blob store, verifies the MAC and decrypts the plaintext.
